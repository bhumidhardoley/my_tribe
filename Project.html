Mishing
Tribe

The Mishing tribe is one of the largest tribal communities in Assam. They primarily live in Majuli, Dhemaji, Lakhimpur, and Tinsukia districts. Originally from Arunachal Pradesh, they migrated to the fertile Brahmaputra plains. Their main livelihood is agriculture, focusing on rice and mustard farming. Traditional weaving is a significant skill, producing intricate handwoven fabrics. Their language, "Mishing," belongs to the Sino-Tibetan Tani group.

TMPK

TMPK (Tangsa Mishing Parishad Kendra) represents the Mishing tribe in Assam. It aims to preserve and promote Mishing culture, traditions, and language. The organization advocates for the rights and welfare of the Mishing community. TMPK organizes cultural events like the Ali-Aye-Ligang festival. It encourages the youth to connect with their roots while embracing modern education. TMPK promotes unity and solidarity within the Mishing community. It works on environmental conservation, emphasizing the importance of natural resources. TMPK supports traditional art forms such as weaving and folk music. The organization ensures the preservation of Mishing cultural identity. TMPK continues to strengthen the Mishing community and represent their interests.
